Sea-Level Rise

Sea-Level Rise :

As global temperatures rise, polar ice caps and glaciers melt, causing sea levels to rise. This can lead to coastal erosion and more frequent coastal flooding.

Causes :

Sea level rise is caused primarily by two factors related to global warming:
The added water from melting ice sheets and glaciers
2.The expansion of seawater as it warms.

Prevention :

Maintain and Restore Wetlands
1.Allow coastal wetlands to migrate inland (e.g., through setbacks, density restrictions land purchases) ...
2.Establish rolling easements.
3.Remove hard protection or other barriers to tidal and riverine flow (e.g., riverine and tidal dike removals)
