Man-Made Disasters

Man-Made Disasters :

Man-made disasters, also known as anthropogenic disasters, are events or incidents that result from human actions, negligence, or intent and lead to significant harm, damage, or disruption to people, property, and the environment. These disasters can vary in scale and impact, but they all share the common element of being caused or exacerbated by human activities.
Man-made disasters can be categorized into several types:

  1. Industrial Accidents
  2. Environmental pollution
  3. Technological Failures
  4. Terrorism
  5. War and Conflict
  6. Cybersecurity Incidents
  7. Human error
  8. Infrastructure Failures
  9. Social and Political factors
